# Our Philosophy

[README](../README.md) | [License](../LICENSE) | [Comparisons](COMPARISONS.md) | [Our Philosophy](OUR_PHILOSOPHY.md)

PixelArt98 exists because creative software should feel like a tool in your hands, not a meter running in the background.

We despise the experience of needing a paid subscription just to keep making images. We despise being pushed into accounts, clouds, locked ecosystems, paid add-ons, and artificial limitations around basic creative work. We especially reject the Adobe-style model where a user can feel surrounded by subscriptions, extensions, bundled services, and format workflows before they even get to make something.

PDF and other common formats are valuable standards. The problem is not that standards exist. The problem is when an ecosystem turns essential formats, extensions, integrations, cloud storage, or simple quality-of-life features into another paid gate.

Our promise is simple:

- Build a fast, understandable, native editor.
- Keep the user experience direct and respectful.
- Make non-commercial use free.
- Avoid dark patterns, forced cloud workflows, and subscription pressure.
- Optimize for real users, including people on older hardware.
- Add advanced capabilities, including GPU acceleration and depth-map generation, only in ways that stay local, understandable, and optional.
- Keep improving until PixelArt98 feels like a serious tool, not a compromise.

Money is not the mission. The mission is to provide the best user experience we can to everyone who wants to create, learn, draw, animate, edit, or experiment without being treated like a revenue stream.

Commercial use still requires permission under the project license so other people cannot take the work, bundle it, sell it, or monetize it without agreement. That protection exists to keep the project free for normal users, not to turn it into the kind of product we are trying to escape.
